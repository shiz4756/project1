Yunnan Lijiang

History

    Lijiang has a long history. It is the ancient birthplace of dongba culture. The naxi people of this city still live in this culture. The city's history can be traced back to the warring states period (475-221 BC) in China. Today, the city is primarily known as a scenic garden resort, with lush green valleys surrounded by snow-capped peaks.

    Lijiang is at a crossroads. It is one of the few towns where the architectural styles of northern and southern China can be found. It marks the place where the Yangtze river leaves Tibet to become the main artery of Chinese commerce and culture. The Yangtze river flows from lijiang into sichuan province, through chongqing, to the huge three gorges dam, to wuhan and nanjing, and to the east China sea near Shanghai.

    There is a canal in lijiang. The city's ancient water distribution system was complex, but still very practical. The canal adds to the beauty of the city.

    In addition to the large naxi ethnic group, lijiang is also inhabited by yi, li, pumi, bai, Dai, miao, zhuang, Tibetan and hui ethnic groups. In 1997, UNESCO listed lijiang as one of the world's major cultural heritage sites.

Lijiang Old Town

    Lijiang Old Town is located in the ancient city of Lijiang City, Yunnan Province. It is located in the middle of the Lijiang Dam. It was built in the late Song Dynasty and early Yuan Dynasty. It is one of the famous historical and cultural cities in China. It is also one of the two ancient cities that have successfully declared the world cultural heritage with the entire ancient city One.

    Lijiang Old Town is a national 5A-level tourist attraction. The scenic spots include Sifang Street, Wufeng Building, Mufu, Wenchang Palace, Heilongtan, Xueshan Academy, Wangjiazhuang Christian Church, Wang Pizhen Memorial Hall, Fang Guoyu's former residence, Baima Longtan Temple, Gu Peter’s old residence, Jinglian Temple, Puxian Temple, etc.

    Lijiang Old Town is also called Dayan Town. It consists of three parts: Dayan, Baisha, and Shuhe. Dayan Ancient City is a relatively representative scenic spot.

    The ethnic minorities in Lijiang Old Town are also very strong, such as Naxi ancient music, Dongba ceremonies, divination culture, ancient town bars, and Naxi torch festival, etc., are entertainment items that cannot be missed when traveling to Lijiang.

    Lijiang Old Town is an ancient city without walls. The smooth and clean bluestone roads, houses made of clay structures, and the ubiquitous small bridges and flowing water form a dynamic landscape with the crowds that come and go, making people feel not rush. More quiet and relaxing.

    The most suitable place to take a panoramic view of Lijiang Old Town is at Wenchang Palace, where the brightly lit night of Lijiang can be photographed.

Lugu Lake

    Lugu Lake is located 200 kilometers (about 124 miles) from the center of Lijiang, at the junction of Ninglang County in Yunnan Province and Yanyuan County in Sichuan Province. It is like a beautiful pearl, shining in the mountains of the northwestern Yunnan plateau.

    The lake is horseshoe shaped-long from north to south and narrow from east to west. The scenery of the lake is constantly changing throughout the day. The morning fog mixes with the rays of the rising sun, giving the lake an orange glow covered by fog. When the sun casts the shadows of the mountains on the water, the lake shows a verdant color. When the sun sets in the evening, the lake level is quiet and dark green, and when the night comes, the lake level is quiet. The breeze was blowing and the water was rippling.

    There are five islands on the lake. They vary in size, like green boats floating on the water. Heiwawu Island, Liwubi Island and Lige Island are the most famous. Heiwaya Island is located in the center of the lake, where various birds live. The smallest island on the lake is Nissi, a rectangular rock covered with shrubs and moss. In addition to the five islands, there are three peninsulas and an island. There is a path paved with small stones leading to the seawall on the coast.

    Mystery and charm surround this lake, partly because it used to be an unspoilt place and still retains most of its natural beauty, but mainly because the Mosuo people living there formed a matriarchal society. No marriage. A man stays at a woman's house as a partner and is called "Axia". This intimate relationship lasts as long as it lasts. The children were brought up by women, using their mother ’s surname. The family is made up of members of matrilineal relatives. Women's production and management have a dominant position in society, forming a modern "women's kingdom", which adds a sense of mystery to this place.

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain

    Jade Dragon Snow Mountain is the southernmost snow mountain in the northern hemisphere. As a must-see attraction in Lijiang City, Jade Dragon Snow Mountain is composed of 13 peaks, of which the highest mountain is 5596 meters above sea level. This mountain range is 35 kilometers long from north to south and 15 kilometers wide from east to west. The snow mountain seen from the ancient city of Lijiang resembles a huge jade dragon, hence the name "Jade Dragon Snow Mountain". Jade Dragon Snow Mountain is also called “Black and White Mountain” because this mountain is mainly composed of white limestone and black flint.

    Jade Dragon Snow Mountain is regarded as a holy mountain by the Naxi people. According to the legend of the Naxi people, it is the incarnation of the three gods of the Naxi people. It has subtropical, temperate and cold natural landscapes such as meadows, glaciers, canyons, waterfalls and flowers. It is also home to many rare animals and plants. In 2007, Yulong Snow Mountain Scenic Area was approved as a National 5a-level scenic spot by the National Tourism Administration.

    The must-visit attractions in Yulong Snow Mountain Scenic Area include Glacier Park, Ganhaizi Meadow, Yak Meadow, Baihe (Blue Moon Valley), and Spruce Meadow. Tourists can walk or rent horses from Ganhaizi to Yak Meadow. In addition, there are three ropeways in the scenic area, which lead to Glacier Park, Spruce Meadow and Yak Meadow.

Ganhaizi Meadow

    Ganhaizi is the gateway of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain. Refers to a large grassland on the east side of the towering Jade Dragon Snow Mountain. The grassland is located at an altitude of 2900 meters, 4 kilometers long and 1.5 kilometers wide. Gan Haizi is a natural pasture. In spring, Tibetan, Yi, and Naxi herdsmen wear felt hats, ride on horseback, and graze cattle, sheep, and yaks on the grasslands.

Spruce Meadow

    Spruce Meadow is located at the foot of Yulong Snow Mountain. At an altitude of 3240 meters, it is a holy place for the Naxi people. According to legend, this is the gateway to the "Third Jade Dragon", which is considered a utopia. Another legend is that if a young couple commits suicide on Spruce Meadow, their soul will enter the Jade Dragon in the third country and get eternal happiness. Spruce Meadows was also the location of the live show “Impression Lijiang” directed by Zhang Yimou, director of the opening ceremony of the 2008 Olympic Games.

Yak Meadow

    Yak Meadow is called "Bannon country" in Naxi language, which means "beautiful and mysterious paradise" . In summer, the grass is dotted with flowers and yak, presenting the most beautiful and tranquil scenery. From the yak meadow, you can see the 13 peaks of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain.
