The Great Wall

History

    The Great Wall was not built overnight. It was the result of the joint efforts of thousands of people throughout China. These city walls stretch for thousands of miles in total and are at various stages of disrepair or repair. The origin of the Great Wall dates back to the 7th century BC, when many vassal states fought against each other. Each duke country or kingdom has built its own walls to protect itself. In 221 BC, the King of the Qin State conquered other countries, unified the whole country, became the Emperor of the Qin State, and became the Emperor Qin (the first emperor of the Qin Dynasty). Qin Shihuang ordered the city walls to be connected, and the Great Wall of the Qin Dynasty appeared. Few city walls remained in the Qing Dynasty. Later dynasties sometimes repaired and rebuilt along the lines established by their predecessors.

    The Han Dynasty (206 BC-220 AD) was the second climax of the restoration of the Great Wall, because the Great Wall was threatened by the northern Huns at that time. The Great Wall has been reconstructed and maintained for nearly 400 years. Later the Sui, Northern and Jin dynasties repaired, restored or expanded the Great Wall to protect themselves. The Great Wall was rebuilt after the fall of the Yuan Dynasty in the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644). The Ming Dynasty adopted a new strategy to block Mongolians by building city walls on the northern border of China. The Ming dynasty architecture was stronger and more refined, because stones and bricks were used instead of rammed earth. The section near the capital of the Ming Dynasty, Beijing, is particularly strong. When the Ming Dynasty was defeated by the Manchus in 1644, the Great Wall lost its defensive function because the Qing Dynasty (1644-1911) controlled the inside and outside of the Great Wall. Most of the Great Wall we see today are from the Ming Dynasty.

Cultural Value

    The Great Wall is a powerful symbol of Chinese culture. The remains of the Great Wall and its fortress and watchtower show us China's national pride, magnificent engineering and firm resistance, but also its luxurious architecture and artistic culture. The Great Wall inspired many paintings, poetry, operas, stories and legends.

    Today, the Great Wall is a model for protecting cultural heritage and expanding its opening up. The Great Wall has become China's largest tourist attraction. Great Wall painting, embroidery, decoration, photography and postcards are a modern Chinese art form.

    The Great Wall is a powerful symbol. It represents the reunification of China, because it was linked when China was first reunified in the Qin Dynasty (221-206 BC). It represents the extraordinary ability of the Chinese to work together for the benefit of the country.

    On the one hand, the Great Wall represents the unity of China, on the other hand, the Great Wall represents the isolation from the rest of the world, and the efforts a country has made to protect its culture and resist invaders.

     Ironically, as a tourist attraction, the Great Wall is now helping to attract millions of foreigners to China to experience Chinese culture. China welcomes you!

Great Wall Legends and Stories

1. Meng Jiang Nv Weeping Over the Great Wall

    This story of love and devotion was established during the qin Great Wall.

    A girl named Meng Jiangnv saved a man named Fan Xiliang. They fell in love with each other and got married. Unfortunately, Fan Xiliang was found, captured, and returned to forced labor at a Great Wall construction site.

    Meng Jiangnv waited for her husband day and night. Winter came, but Fan Xiliang did not return. The Meng Jiang woman sewed some warm clothes for her husband. When she arrived at the construction site, Fan Xiliang was not seen. She learned that Fan Xiliang was dead and his body was buried in the Great Wall.

    Meng Jiangnv stayed by the wall and cried day and night. Deeply moved by the girl's bitter weeping, the Great Wall collapsed and she finally found Fan Xiliang's body.

    The Temple of Meng Jiangnv can be found 5 km northeast of the Shanhai Pass Great Wall Fortress.

3. The 'Metal Soup' Great Wall - Huanghuacheng

    When the Great Wall was built in the Ming Dynasty, Emperor Yongle (reigned 1402-1424) ordered a general named Cai Kai to build the Huanghua City Great Wall. Cai Kai is meticulous in quality control and completed this task after several years of hard work. However, when the Ministry of War heard of his profligate news, he was hurriedly beheaded.

    After an official found the wall to be particularly strong and steep, the emperor realized that he had faulted Cai Kai by mistake. Later, the emperor built a mausoleum for Cai Kai to commemorate his great contributions, and named this section of the Great Wall "Metal Soup" because it was strong.
